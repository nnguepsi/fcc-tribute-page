FREDERICK DOUGLASS

Abolitionist Leader

Born into slavery in Maryland, Frederick Doublass was an eminent human rights leader in the anti-slavery movement and the first African American citizen to hold a high U.S. government rank, advising presidents and lecturing on causes like women's rights.

  • Taught other slaves how to read
  • Escaped slavery dressed as a sailor
  • Anti-slavery lecturer
  • Published abolitionist newspapers: The North Star, Frederick Douglass Weekly, Frederick Douglass' Paper, Douglass' Monthly and New National Era
  • Vice Presidential candidate
